Goal: lean, tight, high-stamina build
Built around your 06:45–07:30 gym slot and your existing meals. General framework, not a clinical plan — for exact cut/bulk numbers, a trainer or nutritionist who can see your real measurements will get you further.
From what you shared: you're a lean, slim-built frame with low body fat already — the "tight" look you want is mostly a muscle-building job, not a fat-cutting one. That changes the plan: prioritise slightly higher calories with a protein surplus and progressive overload (more reps/weight over time), rather than restriction. Definition follows once there's more muscle underneath, especially chest, shoulders and core.
Daily nutrition
On waking (04:30)
Warm water + soaked seeds
Warm water first, then soaked moringa seeds / methi / chia if available. No sugar drinks.
06:00–06:30 prep → 07:30 after gym
Protein-forward breakfast
Eggs / sprouts / paneer / oats with nuts, eaten right after gym for recovery. This is the meal that builds stamina — don't skip protein here.
Midday
Plate-half rule
Rice okay, but vegetables/salad/curd fill half the plate first, rice second, plus a protein side (fish/chicken/lentils/paneer).
19:00–20:00
Light dinner, early cutoff
2 chapathis + vegetable/dal. Finish by ~20:00–20:30 so digestion doesn't fight sleep quality.
Through the day
Hydration + your own moringa
3L water spread through the day. A teaspoon of your own MURI moringa powder in warm water or smoothie is a solid stamina/micronutrient add — you're already sourcing it.
Workout — limited equipment (45 min, 06:45–07:30)
Assumes dumbbells, a resistance band, a pull-up bar and bodyweight — no machines needed.
Mon — Push (chest, shoulders, triceps)
  • Dumbbell floor press or push-ups — 4 sets
  • Band/dumbbell shoulder press — 3 sets
  • Dips on a sturdy chair/bench — 3 sets
  • Plank finisher — 3 rounds
Tue — Pull (back, biceps)
  • Pull-ups (assisted with band if needed) — 4 sets
  • Dumbbell or band rows — 3 sets
  • Dumbbell curls — 3 sets
  • Dead hang for grip/back — 2 rounds
Wed — Legs & core (builds the "tight" midsection)
  • Goblet squats (dumbbell) — 4 sets
  • Walking lunges — 3 sets
  • Glute bridges — 3 sets
  • Hanging knee raises / plank — 3 sets
Thu — Stamina / conditioning
  • Jump rope or high-knee sprints — 6 x 1 min, 30 sec rest
  • Burpees — 4 sets of 10
  • Mountain climbers — 4 sets of 30 sec
  • Slow-breathing cooldown — 3 min (recovery quality matters as much as effort)
Fri — Full-body circuit
  • Push-ups, rows, squats, plank — circuit style, 3–4 rounds
  • Finish with band shoulder work
Sat / Sun — Mobility + light stamina
  • Light jog or brisk walk, stretching, band mobility work
  • Keeps recovery active without adding fatigue before your business sprint hours
Brain focus & stamina — daily habits
Fuel for focus
Walnuts, flaxseed, fish (omega-3), and your moringa powder support mental clarity. Green tea instead of a second coffee mid-morning keeps focus steadier without a crash.
Protect sleep — it's the real "brain" lever
Your 04:30 wake-up only works long-term if you're actually asleep by ~22:30–23:00. No phone in the last 20 minutes before bed — screen light right before sleep is the single biggest thing quietly cutting into next-day focus.
2-minute reset between work blocks
Before switching from office work to business work (or business to GF time), 10 slow breaths or a short walk resets attention better than pushing straight through — small thing, compounds over a month.
