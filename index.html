Diet framework — lean & tight, built around your routine
05:15 — on waking
Warm water + soaked seeds
Warm water first, then a small bowl of soaked moringa seeds/methi or chia if available — supports digestion before the day starts. Skip sugar drinks entirely.
~07:30–08:00
Protein-forward breakfast
Eggs / sprouts / idli-sambar / oats with nuts — prioritise protein over pure carbs so you stay full till lunch and don't snack mid-morning at office.
Office lunch
Rice — smaller portion, plate-half rule
Keep rice, but fill half the plate with vegetables/salad/curd first, rice second. Add a protein side (fish/chicken/paneer/lentils). This alone is the single biggest lever for a tighter midsection without cutting rice out.
13:00–13:30
Gym slot
Train close to lunch — protein from lunch supports recovery. Keep a shaker of water/electrolyte with you for this slot.
19:00–20:30
Light dinner — 2 chapathis
Chapathi + vegetable curry/dal, avoid a second rice meal in the same day. Finish eating at least 2 hours before sleep (by ~21:00) so digestion doesn't fight your sleep quality.
Through the day
Hydration
12 glasses (~3L) spread across the day, more on gym days. Water before meals reduces overeating at lunch — use the water tracker on the Today tab.
This is a general framework, not a clinical diet plan — it's built to slot into the schedule you already run (rice at lunch, 2 chapathis at dinner, gym at lunch break) without adding decision fatigue. If you want it tightened further with exact portions or a cut phase, that's worth a conversation with a nutritionist or trainer who can see your actual numbers.
